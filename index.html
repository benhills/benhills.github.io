Benjamin Hills

Cryo and Hydro Geophysicist

Vista Clara Inc.




I am a geophysicist interested in processes at the interface between climate and geologic systems. In particular, my research has focused on the fluid dynamics and solid mechanics of water and ice as Earth materials. I use geophysical methods, such as ice-penetrating radar, to survey glaciers and ice sheets, their geometry, flow, ice properties and subglacial water, and the associated implications for sea-level rise. I also have growing interests in planetary ice and planetary analogs on Earth. I believe in and practice open science, with free access to data, code, and manuscripts.

Teaching

I enjoy teaching from Jupyter Notebooks. I use Binder, so each of the links to the right takes you to a ‘hub’ with a precompiled anaconda environment and jupyter notebooks executable on a remote server.
