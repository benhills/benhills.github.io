Crystal Orientation Fabric

Jan 9, 2022

Past ice flow can be preserved in the ice crystal orientation fabric, especially in the case an ice divide which persists in a steady location (within an ice thickness) for 10’s of thousands of years.

Snow crystals are deposited at random orientation on the glacier surface, meaning the COF is initially isotropic. As ice flows, crystal c-axes rotate away from the primary extensional axis and toward compression (Alley, 1992). In places where ice flow is consistent for thousands of years, the bulk COF becomes anisotropic and can strongly influence any subsequent flow. Single-pole fabrics with upward-pointing c-axes develop at an ice divide where flow is longitudinal extension. In this case, the ice hardens in the vertical and preserves old ice near the bed (Raymond, 1983). Girdle fabrics develop in an ice-stream shear margin where transverse shear dominates. This fabric softens the ice and promotes faster flow (Jackson and Kamb, 1997).
